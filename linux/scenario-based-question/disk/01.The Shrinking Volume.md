# The Scenario 
You are a DevOps Engineer managing a production server. A monitoring alert (Prometheus/Grafana) just fired, notifying you that the /var/lib/docker partition is at 95% capacity. This partition is where all your Docker images and container data are stored.

You check the system and find that the disk is managed via LVM. You have an extra 50GB of unallocated space in your Volume Group (VG) named vg_data.

# The Question
What are the exact steps and commands you would use to increase the size of the Logical Volume (LV) named lv_docker by 20GB and ensure the filesystem also recognizes the new size without unmounting it (keeping the containers running)?


# The answrs
1. First checkout how much space i have in my disk through:
`sudo vgdisplay`
and then extend the space 20GB
`sudo lvextend -L +20GB lv_docker`

2. Now resize the lv
`sudo resize2fs lv_docker`

3. Now check the space 
`df -h`
