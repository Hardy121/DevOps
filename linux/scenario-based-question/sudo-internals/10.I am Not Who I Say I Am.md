**The Scenario:** 

You are a Lead Admin. You want a junior admin (`jr_admin`) to be able to restart the webserver, but specifically as the `web_manager` user, **not** as root. You add this line:
`jr_admin ALL=(web_manager) /usr/bin/systemctl restart nginx`

The junior admin tries to run `sudo systemctl restart nginx` and it fails with:
`sudo: sorry, a password is required`
They type their password, but it still fails.

**The Question:**

What is the specific **command flag** the junior admin is missing to tell `sudo` they want to act as `web_manager` instead of the default `root`?

**The Answer :** 

`sudo -u web_manager systemctl restart nginx`
