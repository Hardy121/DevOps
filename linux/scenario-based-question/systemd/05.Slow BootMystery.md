# The Scenario
Imagine you are managing a fleet of 100 Linux nodes in AWS. You notice that when these nodes auto-scale (start up), they take 3 minutes to become "Ready." In DevOps, every second counts—slow boot times mean your users wait longer during a traffic spike.

You suspect one of the systemd services is hanging or taking too long to initialize.

Your Goal:
Use systemd's built-in "profiling" tools to find the bottleneck.

# The Question
1. What is the base command (starting with systemd) that gives you a high-level breakdown of how long the kernel took vs. the userspace to boot?

2. Which specific sub-command shows you a list of all services sorted by the time they took to initialize?

3. If you wanted to see a visual "tree" showing which services are waiting on others, which sub-command would you use?

# The Answer
1. `systemd-analyze time`
2. `systemd-analyze blame`
3. `systemd-analyze critical-chain`
